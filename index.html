Project

Project Name : Analysis of Renal Stone Calcium Oxalate Monohydrate crystals and evaluation of Antiurolithiatic potential of ethanolic extract of alpinia zerumbet

Abstract Renal stone formation, predominantly composed of Calcium Oxalate Monohydrate (COM) crystals, is a prevalent urological disorder affecting a significant portion of the global population. This study aimed to analyze the morphology and characteristics of COM crystals and evaluate the antiurolithiatic potential of the ethanolic extract derived from Alpinia zerumbet. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) and Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR) were employed to examine the structural features and composition of the COM crystals, respectively. The ethanolic extract of Alpinia zerumbet was subjected to phytochemical screening to identify its bioactive constituents. Further, in vitro assays were conducted to assess the inhibitory effects of the extract on COM crystal formation and aggregation. The results demonstrated that the ethanolic extract of Alpinia zerumbet exhibited significant antiurolithiatic properties by inhibiting COM crystal nucleation and aggregation. The phytochemical analysis revealed the presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which could be responsible for the observed inhibitory effects. These findings suggest that Alpinia zerumbet may serve as a potential natural remedy for the prevention and management of renal stone formation.
